How to Write a Successful Cover Letter

by Alexander Strauss

Posted on November 21, 2017 at 1:30 PM

markgranitz/Flickr

Are you ready to write a cover letter that will score you a job? The answer is yes.

It could be the reason your resume gets chosen or pushed aside. So it makes sense to put a lot of effort and time into writing an effective cover letter. All the information you need to know to write a successful cover letter is right here. We have tips on what to include in your cover letter, how to format it, and different examples of professionally written cover letters.

A cover letter is a document sent with your resume to provide additional information on your skills and experience. Don’t simply repeat what’s on your resume, a cover letter should include specific information on why you fit the employer’s job description the best. A cover letter is not always needed when applying somewhere, but if you include one with your resume it will show you’ve put some extra effort into your application. It could get your application notcied more than others.

“People who are unable to motivate themselves must be content with mediocrity, no matter how impressive their other talents.”

Andrew Carnegie

A cover letter should complement your resume not repeat what it has. The purpose is to add a personal touch to your application while also interpreting the information in your resume. Effective cover letters explain why you are interested in the company and identifying your skills and experience. Match your skill set to the ones required by the job description. Show where you applied your skills and how they would be effective in the position you applied for. Make sure to make a good first impression, something with a small error or no personality can get you knocked off the potential list of employment right away.

Specifics of what to write - Header, contact info., salutation, introduction(what job you’re applying for, how u heard about it, briefly mention skills and experiences mentioned later, and goal is to get the reader’s attention), body (interested in job and why you’re right for the position), and closing (restate everything)
